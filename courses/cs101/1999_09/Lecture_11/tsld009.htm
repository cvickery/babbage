Addresses of Instructions

  • The computer has a register called the Program Counter (PC) that is used to hold the address of (“a pointer to”) the next bytecode to be executed. Think of it as an index into the Method Area.

  • Calling a method saves a copy of the PC on the Thread’s stack, and puts the address of the method into the PC

  • Returning from a method restores the saved copy of the PC from the Thread’s stack.
