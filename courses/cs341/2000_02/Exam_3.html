Exam ID 4853

Questions 1 through 6 deal with the timing diagram.

NOTE: Parts of Figures 3-37, 4-2, 4-5, and 4-11 were distributed with this exam.  The labels were removed from the timing diagram, and the time intervals were changed to the letters A, B, C …

1.             What two points in the diagram represent the response time of the memory?  (An example of the kind of answer you are to give would be, “from the end of C to the beginning of D,” but that’s not the correct two points.)

2.             What is the name of the bottom signal in the diagram, and what purpose does it serve?  Tell whether it is asserted by the CPU or by the memory.

3.             Interval A is the address delay.  Is the value for that a minimum or a maximum?  Is the address delay a constraint that the CPU must meet, or is it a constraint that the memory must meet?

4.             Assume A is 4 nsec, B is 8 nsec, C is 2 nsec, and E is 3 nsec.  Assume a clock speed of 50 MHz.  What is the slowest memory that could be used which would operate with one wait state?  Hint: Not all these numbers are relevant.

5.             With the same timing assumptions, what is the slowest memory that could be used so the data would be available at the falling edge of T2 instead of at the end of T3 (no wait states)?

6.             Which two intervals in the figure represent “setup” and “hold” times?

7.             Match the busses with “reasonable” values for their speeds.

                Busses: Cache (backside) Bus, ISA Bus, PCI Bus, Memory (frontside) Bus, Q44 (the one that runs on Horace Harding Boulevard) Bus.

                Speeds: 33 Miles per hour, 66 MHz, 100 MHz, 8.3 MHz, 500 MHz

8.             How does a CPU communicate with a device controller?  What types of registers are there in a device controller, and what are they used for?  How does a device controller indicate that it has completed an I/O operation?

9.             Suppose a CPU has a level 1 cache and a level 2 cache, with access times of 5 nsec and 10 nsec, respectively.  The main memory access time is 50 nsec.  If 15% of the accesses are level 1 cache hits and 80% are level 2 cache hits, what is the average access time?

10.           What is the bandwidth of a 32-bit PCI bus operating at 66 MHz?  Be sure to indicate the proper unit of measure for your answer.

11.           Draw a diagram that shows the connections between bit i of the TOS register and the two busses of the IJVM.  Be sure to show where the control signals come from and where else they go.

12.           Write a sequence of microinstructions that will duplicate the top of stack.  That is, it pushes a copy of the value on the top of the stack onto the stack.  The stack pointer register ends up incremented by one, but the TOS register does not change.

13.           What are the purposes of the following registers: MAR, MDR, PC, MBR?

14.           What does the following sequence of microinstructions do if they are executed one after the other?  Tell what registers and/or memory locations change and what they are changed to.

                                PC = PC + 1; fetch

H = MBRU << 8

                                H = MBRU OR H

15.           Translate each of the microinstructions in Question 14 to binary.  Assume the first microinstruction is the first one to be executed for invokevirtual instructions (has label “invokevirtual1”).  You pick the addresses for the other two; tell what addresses you chose and why.
