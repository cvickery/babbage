Simplifying Networks
Minimization
• Algebraic
– Pair terms that differ in exactly one literal, which must
be the same variable in its asserted and complemented
form (such as x’).
–  x•1 = x
–  x+x’= 1
– So, x •(y+y’) = x
• Karnaugh Map
– Same idea as algebraic, except works from truth table.
– Arrange truth table so rows that differ in one literal are
adjacent to each other.
– Adjacent minterms that number a power of two can be
combined into one simpler term.
