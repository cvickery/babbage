Tristate Gates
• Output is either a normal logic value, or a special
third state, in which the output is effectively
disconnected from the circuit.
– An extra input determines whether the output is a logic
value or is “tristated” (also called “floating”). 1 means
logic; 0 means float.
– Makes it possible to connect output of gates together
without causing short circuits.
– No more than one tristate gate in a group may generate
a logic value at a time.
• Decoders are good for controlling this.
