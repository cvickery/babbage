Encoding Audio Information
A transducer converts sound pressure to voltage.
Microphone.
Voltage is periodically sampled and converted to binary numbers by an Analog to Digital Converter (ADC) circuit.
Nyquist Theorem: Sampling rate must be two times the highest frequency to be reproduced.
Human auditory sensitivity ends at about 20KHz.
CD standard is 44KHz per channel.
Number of bits per sample determines how many different pressure levels can be reconstructed.
CD standard is 12 bits per sample.

Decoding Audio Information
Sequence of binary numbers are entered into a Digital to Analog Converter (DAC) circuit in real time.  (“Real-time” depends on original sampling rate.)
A transducer converts the voltages generated by the DAC into sound pressure.
Speakers, earphones.
