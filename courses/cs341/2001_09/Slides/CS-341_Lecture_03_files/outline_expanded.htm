Sign Magnitude
Two zeros
Number line changes direction in the middle
Addition may involve subtraction, depending on the signs of the operands
Simple for people to understand
Used for the sign of IEEE-754 floating-point numbers

Biased
Numerical values are offset by (approximately) half the range.
Values increase monotonically, making it good for comparing sizes of encoded values.
Addition requires subtraction of the extra copy of the bias in the sum
Used for the exponent field of IEEE-754 floating point numbers

One’s Complement
Not used in computing, but useful as an approximation to two’s complement.
Negation by inverting bits.
Two zeros.
Have to add the carry out of the left end to the sum when doing sums.  (“End-around carry.”)

Two’s Complement
Virtually always used for fixed-point arithmetic
Integer is special case of fixed-point
One zero
Monotonic, with discontinuity between zero and largest negative value
Range for n bits is –2n-1 to +2n-1-1
Negate by complementing and adding one
Works for negative, positive, and zero, but not smallest negative value
Sign bit has a negative weight
Sign Extension
Ignore leftmost carry when adding
Subtract by adding the negation of the subtrahend (bottom number) from the minuend (top number)
Overflow detected when carry into sign bit differs from the carry out

Floating Point
Based on scientific notation
Value = ± mantissa * base ±exponent
The base is constant (typically two or 16) and is not explicitly encoded.
Left-to-right binary fields in a floating-point value:
Sign of mantissa (sign-magnitude; 0 = +)
Signed exponent (biased encoding)
Mantissa (fraction) value
