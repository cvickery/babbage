Information Measurement
• Shannon’s Theorem
– Information can be measured as uncertainty reduction
– One bit (binary digit) is the amount of uncertainty that
can be reduced by answering one yes/no question.
– Number of bits is log2 of the number of possibilities.
• Guess a number between 0 and 31
– 5 bits
• Guess a day of the week
– Log27 = 2.807
» Sometimes it takes 3 guesses, sometimes 2
» Average number of guesses will be 2.807
• Guess the face of a die
– Log26 = 2.585
» Trick die provides 1.585 bits of information!
